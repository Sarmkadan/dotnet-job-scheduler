# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the dotnet-job-scheduler community and its projects will experience an environment free of harassment, discrimination, and exclusion.

## Our Values

- **Inclusivity**: We welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities
- **Respect**: We treat each other with kindness and professionalism
- **Collaboration**: We value diverse perspectives and work together toward shared goals
- **Integrity**: We conduct ourselves with honesty and accountability

## Expected Behavior

All members of our community are expected to:

### In Interactions with Others

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be respectful of differing opinions, experiences, and viewpoints
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully and offer it thoughtfully
- Focus on what is best for the community
- Show empathy towards other community members
- Assume good intent in communications
- Listen actively before responding

### In Code and Contributions

- Write clear, well-documented code
- Provide helpful feedback on pull requests
- Respect the time and effort of contributors
- Help others learn and improve their skills
- Give credit where credit is due
- Report issues responsibly and constructively

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable in all community spaces (GitHub, discussions, issues, pull requests, etc.):

- **Harassment**: Unwelcome conduct based on protected characteristics or personal attacks
- **Discrimination**: Prejudice or unfair treatment based on identity, background, or any personal characteristic
- **Hate Speech**: Any communication promoting hatred or violence based on identity
- **Trolling**: Deliberately disruptive or inflammatory comments intended to provoke reactions
- **Spam**: Excessive, repetitive, or unsolicited content
- **Sexual Harassment**: Unwanted sexual advances, comments, or imagery
- **Doxxing**: Sharing private information without consent
- **Exclusion**: Deliberately excluding people or preventing participation
- **Threats**: Any threats of violence or harm
- **Abuse of Power**: Misuse of moderator or maintainer privileges

## Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it. All reports are treated seriously and confidentially.

### How to Report

**For public incidents** (visible on GitHub):
- Comment on the relevant issue or pull request
- Contact a maintainer privately with a link to the problematic content

**For private or sensitive matters**:
- Email rutova2@gmail.com with details of the incident
- Include any relevant screenshots or context
- Provide your contact information for follow-up

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:
- Your contact information (email or GitHub handle)
- Names or descriptions of those involved
- Date and location of the incident
- Description of what happened
- Any witnesses or additional context
- Previous similar incidents (if applicable)
- What outcome you're seeking

## Enforcement

Community members asked to stop unacceptable behavior are expected to comply immediately.

### Consequences

Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in:

1. **Warning**: Private notification of the violation with opportunity to correct
2. **Temporary Restrictions**: Temporary ban from participation (7-30 days)
3. **Permanent Ban**: Removal from the community and blocking from future participation

Decisions are made at the discretion of maintainers and will be proportionate to the violation.

## Appeal Process

If you believe a moderation decision was unfair, you may appeal:

1. Reply to the notification with your appeal
2. Provide additional context or evidence
3. A different community leader will review the appeal
4. Decision will be communicated within 7 days
